George has bought some new things for this trip, and I’m rather vexed about them.  The blazer is loud.  I should not like George to know that I thought so, but there really is no other word for it.  He brought it home and showed it to us on Thursday evening.  We asked him what colour he called it, and he said he didn’t know.  He didn’t think there was a name for the colour.  The man had told him it was an Oriental design.  George put it on, and asked us what we thought of it.  Harris said that, as an object to hang over a flower-bed in early spring to frighten the birds away, he should respect it; but that, considered as an article of dress for any human being, except a Margate nigger, it made him ill.
